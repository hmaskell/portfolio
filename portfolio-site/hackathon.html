24 Hour Hackathon

I designed and co-built an Android app.

For a 24 hour Mental Health Hackathon hosted by Design Lab and Lumohacks, my partner and I hacked together an android app designed to help users track what they've consumed mentally and physically, so that they can identify triggers in their own lives to prevent relapses.

Users answer casually formatted questions, one by one, until they have a thorough overview of what they have consumed mentally and physically that day. This information is synthesized into a short journal-entry-style paragraph. After answering all the questions for that day, users can interact with the calendar to see entries from previous days, and can use this to determine potential triggers from patterns they recognize.

Video Demo

In a podcast by Giant Bomb, one of the hosts was discussing how he was dealing with his anxiety. He had decided to create a massive chart detailing everything he did. He quickly found a correlation between having an anxiety attack and his drinking habits. We wanted to harness this concept and distribute it in an accessible and digestible way to people suffering from depression, so that they can take their mental illness into their own hands, and identify triggers for their specific symptoms.

50% of people with depression will relapse into another episode depending on how many episodes they have had in the past. This means that with every relapse, they are even more likely to have yet another relapse, which leads to more relapses in an endless and ever-increasingly vicious cycle.
